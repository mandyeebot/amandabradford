0→1: How I Built & Sold The League

A product development story — not a CEO story.

The Problem

Dating apps were hot-or-not swiping. No professional context, no intent signals, no privacy from coworkers. I wanted a partner who wasn't intimidated by ambition. I built the app I wished existed.

The Build

Designed and coded the matching algorithm myself — professional verification via LinkedIn, behavioral scoring, network graph signals to block coworkers. Shipped iOS v1 as the sole engineer. Started with 419 alpha testers in SF.

Stack: iOS, ML matching, custom CRM (HTML/CSS/JS)

The Monetization Iteration

Launched free, then tested $20/mo. Realized we needed a real business model. Moved to annual subscriptions at $179/yr — conversion held. Then built 4 tiers up to $999/wk for power users who wanted full database access.

ARPU: $75 → $275  |  ARR: $0 → $16M  |  M1 Retention: 90%

The Scale

Launched in 155 cities across 4 countries in 4 years. Built city launch playbooks, owned marketing strategy and execution, ran A/B tests on conversion and churn. 50% DAU/MAU. $6M EBITDA before exit.

MAU: 300K  |  Cities: 155  |  Total raised: $2.3M

The Exit

Ran a competitive M&A process with Match Group and Bumble simultaneously. Chose Match for the distribution and international infrastructure. Post-acquisition, advised Tinder on ARPU growth and retention strategy.

Return: 13x to investors  |  Acquirer: Match Group ($MTCH)
